Recording Reflection

The Voice of a Movement

The Resurrection City Digital Atlas seeks to reimagine a historical movement and legacy through merging innovation in spatial design and multi-media archives.

We witness history through personal experience, through the archive, and through the stories shared with us. You can find more resources on the oral histories we consulted here.

In preserving and interpreting the history and cultural landscape of Resurrection City, the project team wishes to hear from you, the user! While completely optional, we hope you will consider offering feedback on the Digital Atlas. We look forward to hearing from you. Scroll down to leave us a message.

Reflection Prompts

Did you know the story of Resurrection City? What did you know, how did you know it?

Does the website present enough for you to understand its impact and legacy?

Do you have personal memories or stories shared with you about Resurrection City?

Does the archival media draw you in? What are you curious to know more about? What did you learn or appreciate through exploring the website?

What would you change about the website?

How to Use

Using the record button, speak into your computer (or phone) microphone to share your thoughts on the project. Most laptop microphones are located alongside the keyboard. Speak at close range to the device and the recording system will be able to capture your audio.

You are welcome to record and submit multiple 30-second recordings.

Upon completion of a recording, you will be prompted to enter your name and an email to have a copy sent to you. You can remain anonymous or self-identify.
